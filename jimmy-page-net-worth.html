Similarly, he bought the Tower House from Richard Harris in 1972. The house was built in the 13th-century French style, and William Burges was the architect of this home.

Jimmy also owned the Mill House in Windsor (from 1980 to 2004). It’s been reported that one of the former Led Zeppelin members died in that house.

Guitar Collection

Jimmy Page owns a large collection of some of the most beautiful and eccentric guitars in the world.

In an interview with BBC, he revealed that he had owned more than 1,500 guitars.

Explicitly, he played Gibson Les Paul during most of his career.

Jimmy Page once called his “Number One” Gibson Les Paul as his “mistress and wife…except it doesn’t ask for alimony,” and it is without a doubt one of the most legendary guitars in rock history.

Each guitar in his collection is estimated to be worth more than $1 million.

An unknown customer paid $55,000 for the first Gibson Jimmy Page Signature Les Paul guitar.

Furthermore, the initial run of 25 signed guitars went on sale and raised more than a million dollars.

His other legendary guitar collection includes the 1959 Fender Telecaster. The market value of this masterpiece is $25,000. 

However, Jimmy’s Tele was the one that he used to record the solo and the riff of the famous Stairway to Heaven.  

Therefore, if Jimmy put this guitar into the auction, it would bring him millions.

In addition to it, he also owns other popular electric guitars including, 1950s Futurama, 1961 Dan Electro, Number two, Number three, and many more.

1961 Dan Electro was probably famous because he used it to record Kashmir.

Moreover, his acoustic guitar collection is also worth mentioning. It includes Martin D-28, 1963 Gibson J-200, and Harmony Sovereign H-1260.

Jimmy’s acoustic guitars collection is alone worth more than $250,000.

Jimmy Page | Career

Jimmy Page began his musical career as a studio musician in his hometown of London.

He was a member of the rock band The Yardbirds from 1966 to 1968.

Page formed Zeppelin after The Yardbirds disbanded and ruled the stage until 1980.

Outside of his band, Jimmy Page has only released one solo album, Outrider, as of 2021.

After Led Zeppelin disbanded,  Jimmy Page and Robert Plant released two studio albums.

In addition, the musician has a significant impact on the rock & roll community.
