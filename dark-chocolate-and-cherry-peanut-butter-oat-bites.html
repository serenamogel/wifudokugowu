Dark, bittersweet chocolate and dried cherries contribute a nice amount of antioxidants to these sweet little bites. Peanut butter, oats, and rice crispy cereal make them both crunchy and filling. Kind of perfect huh?

I rolled the balls in chopped peanuts but you could also roll them in crushed corn flakes or a different type of nut. It’s really important to squish the mixture well before forming into a ball. Don’t be too gentle or the balls will just fall apart. You can make these any size you choose. Smaller actually works better for the little ones.

Dark Chocolate Cherry Peanut Butter Oat Bites

Dark chocolate and cherry peanut butter oat bites make the perfect snack!

Course Appetizer Cuisine American Keyword dark chocolate cherry peanut butter oat bites Prep Time 10 minutes minutes Cook Time 5 minutes minutes Total Time 15 minutes minutes Servings 18 Calories 145kcal Author Trish - Mom On Timeout

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2/3 cup creamy peanut butter I used Skippy Natural
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 1 cup quick oats
  • 1 1/2 cups crispy rice cereal
  • 1/3 cup dried cherries chopped OR cherry flavored dried cranberries
  • 1/3 cup bittersweet chocolate chips or dark chocolate chips or pieces - chopped
  • 1/4 cup dry-roasted peanuts finely chopped

Instructions

  • Melt butter in a saucepan over medium-low heat.

  • Remove from heat and stir in peanut butter, sugar, vanilla, honey oats, and rice cereal.

  • Let mixture cool for about 20 minutes. (Don't want the chocolate to melt.)

  • Stir in cherries and chocolate chips.

  • With a heaping tablespoon of mixture, form into balls pressing firmly to keep balls together.

  • Roll balls in chopped peanuts, pressing lightly to coat.

  • Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

  • Notes: I used my food processor to chop the peanuts. Just pulse until they reach the desired consistency.

  • Yields 18 balls.

Notes

Recipe adapted from All You Magazine.
